Gzashi-Travel App

Role & Context

UI/UX Designer

Individual Project

Duration: 1 months

Figma Link

Project Overview

"Gzashi" is an all-in-one travel companion app that simplifies trip planning and documentation. Users can create personalized itineraries, access real-time weather updates, discover locations, and receive tailored travel suggestions. The app features an intuitive interface for managing trips, viewing saved destinations, and documenting travel experiences through photos and notes.

Problem

Today's travelers struggle with managing multiple apps for different aspects of their journey - weather apps, mapping services, planning tools, and photo storage. This fragmentation creates a disjointed experience where crucial information is scattered across various platforms, making it difficult to access necessary details quickly.

User Persona

How the Persona Was Created

1

I thought about the target users for my project and imagined a typical traveler who values convenience and technology in their travel planning

2

I read travel blogs, user reviews of travel apps, and forum posts to understand common challenges and preferences of travelers.

3

Based on what I learned, I identified important traits, goals, and pain points that a user like Sarah might have.

4

Using these insights, I created Sarah Turner, a fictional character who represents my target audience. Her background, goals, and behaviors reflect the kind of user I want to design for.

User Flow

Login/Register Page

What I did

I designed login and account creation pages that change based on the season and time of day. This makes the app feel more personal and fun to use

The Idea

The goal was to create a unique and welcoming experience for users. I wanted the pages to feel like they fit the moment—whether it’s summer, winter, day, or night.

Why It’s Special

This project was all about making the user experience more enjoyable. By matching the look of the pages to the time of day or season, the app feels more connected to the user.

What’s Next

I’m excited to keep exploring ways to bring creativity and personalization into my designs.

Main Page

Overview

1

Clean search interface with destination and date inputs. Blue-orange palette chosen for trust and energy. Large search button for clear user action.

2

A preview card displays users' upcoming trips (e.g., Santorini, Greece) with key details like destination, duration, and travel dates. This ensures quick access to important trip information.

3

Curated destinations (Red Beach, Kamari Beach) with ratings and location info. Visual cards maintain clear information hierarchy.

4

Circular icons with minimalist designs provide quick access to categories like Beach, Skiing, and Romantic trips, enabling users to filter options without cognitive overload.

5

Featured blog posts with large, vibrant visuals and descriptive captions offer inspiration and insights for travel planning. These are user-generated to foster a sense of community.

6

Past trips display with dates and budgets (Budapest, Sicily). Helps track experiences and inform future plans.

7

A fixed navigation bar includes essential sections: Home, Explore, Trips, Blogs, and Profile. This ensures quick access to key features, maintaining a cohesive user experience.
